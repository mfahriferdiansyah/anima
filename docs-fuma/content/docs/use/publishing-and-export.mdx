---
title: Publishing and export
description: Publish one note as a link, or take your whole vault out as plain markdown.
---

Your notes are sealed to you by default. When you want something to leave the app, Anima gives you two ways out that keep you in control: publish a single note as a link, or export the whole vault as plain markdown.

## Publish a single note

Publishing makes a separate, public copy of one note that anyone with the link can read. Your original note in the vault stays sealed and untouched; publishing copies, it does not expose your vault. The published copy is a blob your wallet owns, just like the rest of your work.

You choose how open it is.

### Public link

A public link is plain readable text. Anyone who has the link can open the note. Use it for things you genuinely want anyone to see.

### Password-protected link

A password-protected link keeps the note readable only to people who have both the link and the password.

When you publish with a password, the note is encrypted in your own browser using a key derived from that password. The password itself is never uploaded; only the encrypted copy is published. To read it, someone needs the link and the password, and the decryption happens in their browser the same way. Anima never sees the password, and it never holds a plaintext copy of a protected note.

<Callout type="success">
Send the link and the password through different channels (for example, the link by email and the password in a message). Anyone who has both can read the note, so treat the pair like a key.
</Callout>

## Unpublish

Taking a published note down is a real removal, signed by you. Because the published copy is owned by your wallet, unpublishing is an action your wallet signs, and it deletes that public copy. This is the same custody rule as everywhere else in Anima: writing can be delegated, but removing something is yours to sign.

Unpublishing removes the copy you published. It does not touch the original note in your vault.

## Export your whole vault

Export gives you everything as files, not as a proprietary backup. Your vault comes out as a zip of plain markdown: one `.md` file per note, human-readable, openable in any editor.

This is the strongest version of the promise that your notes survive the app. If you ever want to walk away, you leave with readable files, not a format only Anima can open.
