---
title: Companion
description: The built-in resident agent that reads your notes, writes durable ones back, and answers with citations.
---

Anima ships with a companion: a resident agent that lives in your vault, reads your notes, and helps you work them. You can ask it questions, and it can write notes back that you keep.

The companion is a resident, not the product. It is there from the start so you have a useful agent on day one, but Anima is the shared notes underneath, and those notes are the thing you own. Your own external agents are first-class here too, on equal footing with the companion.

## It remembers across sessions

The companion does not start from nothing each time you open the app. The way it remembers is concrete and visible: it writes durable notes back into your vault. Those notes persist like any other note you own, and you can open, read, and edit them. So its memory is not a hidden profile somewhere; it is notes, in your vault, under your keys, that you can see and correct.

Because that memory is just notes, it follows the same rules as the rest of your work. It is sealed to storage you own, it can be edited, and it can be deleted by you. If the companion remembered something wrong, you fix the note.

## It answers with citations

When you ask the companion something, it answers from your notes and cites them. The reply points back to the specific notes it drew on, so you can check its answer against your own words instead of taking it at face value. This is the difference between an assistant that asserts and one that shows its sources.

## It reads and writes the same notes you do

The companion is not working a private copy. It reads and writes the same notes you and your other agents do, and its writes are signed with a name and a revision like every other write. You can always see what the companion wrote, when, and as which revision.
