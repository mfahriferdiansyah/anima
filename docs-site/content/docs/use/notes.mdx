---
title: Notes
description: Capture, search, edit, and forget notes you actually own.
---

A note in Anima is plain text and markdown. Underneath, each note is encrypted and stored as a blob that your wallet owns, so the note is yours to keep, change, and forget on your own terms.

## Capture

Write a note and save it. Short or long, a single line or a full page, it is the same kind of thing: text you own. Saving encrypts the note and stores it under your keys. Your own agents and the companion can read and add notes to the same vault, and every write carries a name and a revision so you can always see who wrote what.

## Search

Find notes across your vault when you need them back. Search looks over the notes you have, including the ones your agents wrote, so retrieval is one move rather than scrolling.

## Edit

Open a note and change it. Edits are versioned: each save advances the note's revision, so a note has a history rather than silently overwriting itself. The newest version is what you read; the revision is part of what gets signed when an agent or a teammate writes.

## Forget

Forgetting a note in Anima is real, not a hide. Delete is the everyday word for it; the action underneath is stronger.

Because each note's blob is owned by your wallet, forgetting it is something only your wallet can sign. Agents that write to your vault cannot destroy your notes: writing and forgetting are separated on purpose, and forgetting sits with you. When you forget a note, your wallet signs to remove its blob, an action auditable on the chain rather than a flag flipped on a server you have to trust to honor it.

<Callout type="warn">
A forget is final by design. It is wallet-signed and removes the only retrievable, renewable copy of the note. The bytes were Seal-sealed to your wallet the whole time, so there is nothing an operator could read while the note existed and nothing one can restore once it is gone. If you might want a note later, [share](/use/publishing-and-export) or [export](/use/publishing-and-export) it first.
</Callout>
