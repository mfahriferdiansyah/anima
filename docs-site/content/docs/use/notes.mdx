---
title: Notes
description: Capture, search, edit, and delete notes you actually own.
---

A note in Anima is plain text and markdown. Underneath, each note is encrypted and stored as a blob that your wallet owns, so the note is yours to keep, change, and remove on your own terms.

## Capture

Write a note and save it. Short or long, a single line or a full page, it is the same kind of thing: text you own. Saving encrypts the note and stores it under your keys. Your own agents and the companion can read and add notes to the same vault, and every write carries a name and a revision so you can always see who wrote what.

## Search

Find notes across your vault when you need them back. Search looks over the notes you have, including the ones your agents wrote, so retrieval is one move rather than scrolling.

## Edit

Open a note and change it. Edits are versioned: each save advances the note's revision, so a note has a history rather than silently overwriting itself. The newest version is what you read; the revision is part of what gets signed when an agent or a teammate writes.

## Delete

Deletion in Anima is a real forget, not a hide.

Because each note's blob is owned by your wallet, removing it is an action only your wallet can sign. Agents that write to your vault cannot destroy your notes; writing and deleting are separated on purpose, and deletion sits with you. When you delete, the note's blob is removed from storage. That removal is an on-chain action your wallet signs, auditable on the chain, not a flag flipped on a server you have to trust to honor it.

<Callout type="warn">
A real delete is permanent. Once the note's blob is gone there is no server-side copy to restore from. If you might want a note later, [publish](/use/publishing-and-export) or [export](/use/publishing-and-export) it before you delete.
</Callout>
