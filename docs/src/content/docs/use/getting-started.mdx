title: Getting started
description: What Anima is for a note-taker, and the shape of your first session.
---

Anima is a place for your notes. You write them like a page or lay them out on a canvas, and they are yours: each one is sealed to storage you own, so your notes outlive any single app, including this one.

What makes Anima different from a plain notes app is that your own ai tools work the same notes you do. The companion that ships with Anima reads and writes your notes, and your own external agents (claude code, codex, a teammate's agent) can connect to the same vault and do the same. People and agents work one shared set of notes, and every write is signed with a name and a revision.

## Your first session

You do not need to learn the whole app to get value on day one. A first session is three moves.

### 1. Capture a note

Write something down. A note can be a quick line or a long page; it is plain text and markdown underneath. When you save it, the note is encrypted and stored as a blob that your wallet owns. Nothing about a note lives only on someone else's server.

### 2. Ask the companion

The companion is a resident agent that reads your notes and answers from them. Ask it a question and it replies with citations back to the notes it drew from, so you can check its work against your own words. When something is worth keeping, the companion writes a durable note back into your vault that you can open, read, and edit like any other note.

### 3. Share or export

When a note is ready to leave the app, you have two honest exits:

- **Publish one note** as a link. Public, or password-protected with a key derived in your own browser.
- **Export your whole vault** as a zip of plain markdown files you can read anywhere.

Neither one locks your work inside Anima. That is the point.

## Where to go next

- [Notes](/use/notes/): capture, search, edit, and delete. Deletion is a real forget, signed by your wallet.
- [Publishing and export](/use/publishing-and-export/): publish one note as a link, or take the whole vault out as markdown.
- [Companion](/use/companion/): the built-in agent that remembers across sessions by writing notes back, and answers with citations.

:::note
Your own external agents are first-class here, on equal footing with the built-in companion. If you write code, the [build track](/build/quickstart/) shows how to connect an agent you already use to the same vault.
:::
